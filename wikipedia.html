Albert Einstein

Born 14 March 1879
Died 18 April 1955
Nationality German
Fields Physics
Known for Theory of Relativity

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist widely acknowledged as one of the greatest and most influential physicists of all time. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity, but he also made important contributions to the development of the theory of quantum mechanics.

Early Life

Einstein was born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire, on 14 March 1879. His family moved to Munich shortly thereafter. He had a passion for mathematics and science from an early age.

Scientific Contributions

In 1905, he published four groundbreaking papers in the Annalen der Physik, which brought him to the notice of the academic world. These papers included the theory of special relativity and the famous equation E = mc².

Nobel Prize

He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, a pivotal step in the development of quantum theory.

Legacy

Einstein's work continues to influence the world of physics and beyond. His name is synonymous with "genius," and his image is one of the most recognizable in the world.
